WWII Flight Missions

Military aviation during WWII saw major technological advancements and redefined how war was fought from the sky. The Department of Defense released Theater History of Operations for historic aerial bombings from WWI through Vietnam.

The dataset includes an abundance of information including allied countries that carried out the airstrikes, the countries and territorries that were targeted, the aircraft used, and the ordinance used in the strikes.

Below is an interactive map of the number of missions that targeted each country. In total there were 178,281 missions flown during the war. The United States flew 53% of those missions, followed by Great Britian, New Zealand, then Australia. More than 61,000 missions targeted Germany, specifically Berlin, followed by Italy, France, then Burma, which is now Myanmar. The B17 Bomber flew the majority of the misions (24%). Followed by the B24, B25, A20, then B26.

One interesting note from the map is the airstikes carried out on American soil in the Aluetian Islands campaign, which is a territory in Alaska and was part of the Pacific Campaign.

The second chart below shows a breakdown of flight missions year over year. As you can see 61% of all flight missions flown in WWII occurred in 1944. WWII ended on September 2, 1945 and obviously, the nuclear bombings by the U.S. on Japan played a significant role in ending the war, but it seems highly possible the dramatic increase in bombings in 1944 also played a role in Germany's retreat and ending the conflict.
